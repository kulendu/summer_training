ABOUT THE PROJECT


The topic of our problem is “Juvenile Delinquency”.Juvenile crime occurs when minors violate a criminal statute. When a juvenile commits a crime, the procedure that takes place differs from that of the adult offender. While it is common for the normal citizens to consider ages between 10-18 as minors but the justice system considers minors even younger as adults, if the crime committed is very serious. Juvenile crime is also known as Juvenile Offending or youth crime is the participation in the illegal behavior of the minors. Juvenile crime is the legal definition rather than specific behavior or psychiatric syndrome. A juvenile is person detention who is typically under the age of 18 and commits an act that otherwise would have been charged as a crime if they were adult. Juvenile crime usually is the participation of the minors of the age between 10-17 years, in illegal behavior or activities.

HOW IS THE PROBLEM RELEVANT FOR THE SOCIETY ?

Juvenile delinquency or Juvenile crime is the participation of a minor child, in illegal behaviour or activities. Juvenile delinquency is also used to refer to children who exhibit a persistent behaviour of mischievousness or disobedience, so as to be considered out of parental control, becoming subject to legal action by the judicial system.


Juvenile delinquency is an enormous problem in India by which most of the youth ruin their lives. Because of juvenile crime and related problems youth, their families and the entire society suffer multiple consequences. Not only does the problem affect the victims of the crime, but it also affects the juvenile delinquents family, their future, and the society as a whole. The most obvious people affected by juvenile delinquency are the victims. The most profound consequence of crimes committed by juveniles carries due to socio-economic and psychological problems which reflect on their family members and the society. Due to the psychological problems, sometimes juveniles involved in robberies, rapes and assaults also are significant. With these criminal activities the juveniles habituate to consume alcohol or other drugs. The main objective of this paper is to study the incidence of juvenile delinquency with reference to psychological perspectives. The juvenile who commit serious crimes challenge their future to protest perceived abuses that have been perpetrated against them. This makes them psychological depression and in turn reflects to commit more crimes. In this circumstance the study on incidence of juvenile delinquency is very important to analyse the causes with reference to psychological perspectives and annihilate in the society.

REASON 1

Some studies have shown reductions in delinquent behaviour when a teenager drops out of school or when there is no proper amount of education given to the child. When a child or teen is being physically abused at home it is not unusual for them to act out when away from home. This is generally in the form of more violent crimes against people or property. And if they are in the wrong peer group then it is not the people who will help the child to shape into a wrong person, but their intensions will definitely do. So we can solve this problem by making them to stay away from these peer groups, but moreover the people involved in these peer groups are also child belonging to different age groups, so we must also try to teach them what’s right and what’s wrong. This way not only the peer pressure will be destroyed but also their mind-set will also be changed.

REASON 4

Juvenile delinquency is more common in poorer neighbourhoods. While all neighbourhoods are not exempt from delinquent activities, it is believed they happen more in areas where children feel they must commit crimes to prosper. Theft and similar crimes may actually be a result of necessity and not that of just a petty crime. The only true help for this situation is to make sure that children in these areas have access to what they need and understand that they do not have to commit a crime to get ahead in life. So the main thing we can do is to make this child take into a kind of rehab centre and give them proper guidance, so that they can get over their cruel surrounding circumstances and give them the right moral support.

REASON 5

According to some studies it is found that the main reason for the juvenile crime is the parental behaviour or how parents behave or teach their children, and most of the child are gets affected due to this. So in this case we have to give moral education to their parents and after that the children needs to be ensured that they receive the right moral knowledge so that they can stop the juvenile crime and become a successful citizen.
